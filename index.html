About of Okoboji

In the state of Iowa there is a town on the eastern shore of West Okoboji Lake called Okoboji, located in Dickinson County. It is also called, The Iowa Great Lakes. Okoboji is a massive tourist area in the summer time. People from around the world come to see the beautiful glacier lakes and everything else Okoboji has to offer. Thanks to Spirit Lake and Arnolds Park contributing to Okoboji area there has been an increase in restaurants, stores, resorts etc. This caused Okoboji to become a recognizable name amongst Iowa and other states due to a variety of products being sold.

Seasonal Activities

Summer Activities

In the summer of Okoboji there is a wide variety of things to do. If you visit Okoboji for the lakes. Visitors are able to rent out paddle boards, kayaks, boats, jet-skis etc. to enjoy themselves on the lake. Visitors from around the world also tend to bring their own automotive for recreational purposes as well. Otherwise there are plenty of parks to visit with the family like Arnolds Park and other nature parks. There is a wide variety of restaurants to eat at as well with amazing food. Other some other popular favorites would be: fishing, bowling, community events, concerts, fireworks, shopping etc.

Fall Activities

In the fall of Okoboji there is multiple things to do. Unlike the summer the tourist season comes ends and there is less visitors. This is the perfect time to take nature walks and enjoy fishing on the lakes with it being overcrowded or to loud. Also hunting is very popular as well.

Winter Activities

In the winter of Okoboji there is a variety of things to do. There is one week of January that visitors come back to enjoy which is, Winter Games. Otherwise many of the locals and visitors enjoy ice fishing, snowmobiling, hunting, sledding etc.

Spring Activities

In the Spring of Okoboji there numerous things to do. It is similar to the fall, but it tends to get a little busier. People during the spring still enjoy fishing throughout the seasonal. Activities on the water begin to take place again and people will begin boating, sailing, swimming etc. again.
